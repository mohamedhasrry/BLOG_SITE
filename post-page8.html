Essential Modern Office Tools for Hybrid Work Success

Collaboration software (Slack, Microsoft Teams), desk booking systems (Officely), project management tools (Asana, Jira), and cybersecurity solutions (LastPass, Norton 360) are essential.

Boosts productivity, improves communication, streamlines workflows, and enhances security.Hardware, software, and infrastructure all play a role in optimizing hybrid workspaces.Organizations adopting hybrid work models and seeking to improve team efficiency.

Flexibility at work isn’t just a nice-to-have anymore, it’s the future of work. So, it’s time to get your office tech in gear and make sure you have all the tools you need to keep things running smoothly, and, most importantly, to give your workforce the right conditions to thrive.

Because here’s the thing: Happy employees are 13% more productive, and while your tech stack is only one piece of the puzzle of employee satisfaction and hybrid workspace well-being, nobody can be happy (or productive!) if the tech they’re using is outdated, complicated to learn, or with a horrible UI.

In this article, we’ll cover the must-have office tools every modern workplace needs, split into categories, and explain what key features you should be looking for.

Those are the software tools that empower your people to perform at their best regardless of their location. When employees aren’t always in the same place at the same time, having the right tech stack is what keeps everything (and everyone) connected and in sync.

Collaboration, creativity, productivity, and overall business success all hinge on having the right tech to support a hybrid environment and different working styles. Because, let’s be honest, not everyone is at their best from 9 to 5, and they don’t need to be. But if you’re offering flexible arrangements to your employees, you need to also provide the right tools for them to do their jobs from anywhere and at any time.

Tech is obviously not just software; it’s also the hardware and infrastructure you provide your team with. In this article, we’ll be focusing on the software, but we quickly wanted to touch on all three components of modern office technology.

Fast and performant laptops are a must for any modern workplace, especially if a large part of your team often works from home.Desktop computers might be a necessity for your physical office, although if you equip everyone with a powerful laptop, you could simply provide monitors, keyboards, headsets, and mice for the office. And, obviously, ergonomic chairs and desks!

If your people are often working from home, give them a sufficient home office budget to make sure they have the right working conditions.

Here, you have anything from cloud-based collaboration tools like Google Workspace to communication platforms like Slack or Microsoft Teams, and from project management tools like Asana and Trello to cybersecurity tech to protect your data and everyone’s passwords.
